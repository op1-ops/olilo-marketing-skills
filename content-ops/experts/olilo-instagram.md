# Expert Panel: Olilo Instagram Content

## Context
- Brand: Olilo — prebiotic fiber syrup by Adaptive Kitchen (founded by Joon Yang + Rich Lim)
- Platform: Instagram (feed posts, carousels, Reels captions)
- Voice: Warm, founder-authentic, real. Not clinical. Not corporate wellness.
- Content pillars: fiber/gut health education (FOS65), lifestyle + recipes, Korean fermentation origin, community
- Target audience: Health-conscious adults 25-45 who are actively cutting sugar and looking for practical, sustainable solutions
- Amazon launch: May 15, 2026
- Tone reference: A founder who solved a real problem talking to a friend — not a brand talking at a customer

## The 6 Experts

1. **Founder Voice Checker** — Does this sound like it came from a real person who built this product? Would Joon actually say this? The bar: a real founder talking to a friend who asked "what is this thing you made?" Checks: no corporate wellness speak, no vague benefit language, no "unlock your potential" framing. Flags: overly polished sentences, generic health claims, anything that sounds like a supplement brand ad from 2018. Signals it's working: specific product details (FOS65, the chicory root sourcing, the syrup format), founder perspective ("we found," "this was the problem we kept hitting"), honest acknowledgment of tradeoffs.

2. **Gut Health Educator** — Is the fiber/gut health content accurate, specific, and taught in plain language? Olilo's edge is education — people don't know what FOS65 is or why prebiotic fiber matters in liquid form. This expert checks: Is the science real and specific (not vague "supports gut health")? Is it explained simply enough that someone who never thought about fiber before gets it? Is it interesting — not a textbook? Checks: FOS65 accuracy, prebiotic vs. probiotic distinction when relevant, claim specificity, no health claims that cross FDA lines. Flags: "may help," "supports digestive wellness," buzzword stacking without explanation.

3. **Korean Origin Story Keeper** — Does content that touches the origin story honor the authentic cultural thread without turning it into a gimmick? The fermentation roots matter — this is real heritage, not a marketing angle. Checks: Is the Korean fermentation connection introduced with context and respect? Does it add meaning or does it feel like a badge? Flags: surface-level "inspired by Korean tradition" without substance, over-leaning on cultural identity as a hook without genuine storytelling. When origin comes up: it should feel like Joon sharing something personal, not a brand playing ethnicity for differentiation.

4. **Recipe + Lifestyle Relevance Scorer** — Is this recipe/lifestyle content genuinely useful or just pretty? Does the swap (using Olilo instead of honey/agave/sugar syrup) make real sense in this recipe? Would someone actually make this? Checks: Recipe simplicity (busy parent energy, not food blogger perfectionism), sugar comparison that's concrete (e.g., "this saves X grams vs. maple syrup"), visual appetite appeal of the described concept, seasonal/timing relevance. Flags: recipes that require 12 ingredients and a stand mixer, forced product placement that ignores flavor logic, "healthy" framing that sounds like diet culture.

5. **Scroll-Stop Visual Concept Reviewer** — Would someone stop for this on a busy Sunday morning scroll? Instagram is fast. Olilo's audience is health-aware but not obsessively so — they want content that earns their pause. Checks: Is the visual concept clear and immediate? Does it communicate the point before anyone reads a word? Does it feel warm (not sterile, not stock-photo clean, not lab-coating aesthetic)? Olilo visual direction: real kitchen settings, natural light, food-forward, human hands, lived-in spaces. Flags: white-background clinical product shots, infographic-heavy carousels with 8-point fonts, stock wellness imagery.

6. **AI Writing Detector (Humanizer)** — 1.5x weight. Checks caption text specifically. Olilo captions fail when they sound like a content calendar, not a person. See `experts/humanizer.md` for the full 24-pattern rubric. Olilo-specific flags: "gut health journey," "nourish from within," "fuel your body," "wellness routine," "made with love," "wholesome goodness," sycophantic openers ("So excited to share..."), significance inflation ("game-changer," "revolutionary"), any sentence that could appear on a Whole Foods shelf tag. Green signals: specific numbers, a real question the founders actually asked themselves, one concrete detail that only someone who made the product would know.

## Scoring Criteria
- **Founder voice** — Reads like Joon talking, not a brand writing
- **Education clarity** — Teaches something real in plain language
- **Cultural honesty** — Origin story is earned, not performed
- **Recipe utility** — Actually useful, not just Instagrammable
- **Visual warmth** — Warm, real, lived-in — not clinical or overly produced
- **Human voice** — No AI slop, no wellness jargon, no corporate polish

## Content Pillar Weighting
Each post should map to one primary pillar. Scoring adjusts context accordingly:

| Pillar | Primary Lens |
|--------|-------------|
| Fiber/gut health education | Gut Health Educator leads; Founder Voice confirms |
| Lifestyle + recipes | Recipe Relevance leads; Visual Concept confirms |
| Korean fermentation origin | Origin Story Keeper leads; Founder Voice confirms |
| Community / user stories | Founder Voice leads; Humanizer scrutinizes hardest |

## What a 90+ Olilo Post Looks Like
- Opens with something specific, not a category claim ("Most syrups have 17g of sugar per tablespoon. Ours has 0.")
- Teaches one thing clearly and simply
- Sounds like a person who actually made the product
- Has a visual concept that earns a pause — not a scream
- Ends with a genuine question or invitation, not "link in bio to shop now"
- Has 4-6 targeted hashtags, not 28 spray-and-pray tags
