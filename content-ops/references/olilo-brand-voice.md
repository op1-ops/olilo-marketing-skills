# Olilo Brand Voice Reference

**Brand:** Olilo — prebiotic fiber syrup by Adaptive Kitchen
**Founders:** Joon Yang + Rich Lim
**Amazon launch:** May 15, 2026
**Product:** FOS65 prebiotic fiber syrup. Zero sugar. Chicory root sourced. Liquid format.

---

## The One-Line Voice Brief

A founder who solved a real problem — talking to a friend who asked "wait, what is this thing you made?"

---

## Who We're Talking To

Health-conscious adults 25-45. They're already trying to cut sugar. They're curious about gut health but not obsessive. They want real solutions, not another supplement brand lecturing them. They're busy — a parent, a professional, someone who cooks but isn't a food blogger. They're skeptical of wellness marketing. They've been burned by products that over-promised.

---

## What Olilo Sounds Like

**DO:**
- Specific numbers over vague claims ("17g of sugar per tablespoon in most syrups" beats "high in sugar")
- Founder-perspective framing ("we kept running into," "the problem we were solving")
- Plain-language science ("FOS65 is a type of prebiotic fiber — your gut bacteria eat it, not you")
- Short sentences. Real pauses. Thoughts that breathe.
- Honest tradeoffs ("it's not as sweet as honey — that's the point")
- Cultural specificity with context ("the fermentation principles behind kimchi and doenjang — that's the tradition this comes from")
- Questions a real person would ask themselves
- One concrete detail that only a founder who made the product would know

**DO NOT:**
- Wellness jargon stacking: "nourish your gut microbiome," "fuel your wellness journey," "wholesome goodness"
- Vague benefit language: "supports digestive health," "promotes gut wellness," "may help with..."
- Corporate enthusiasm: "We're so excited to share," "thrilled to announce," "this is a game-changer"
- Diet culture framing: "guilt-free," "clean eating," "healthy indulgence"
- Over-produced polish: anything that sounds like it was written by an agency for a Whole Foods shelf tag
- Cultural tourism: dropping Korean heritage as a marketing badge without actual context or story
- Instagram cliches: "good vibes only," "made with love," "nourish from within"
- Significance inflation: "revolutionary," "transformative," "life-changing"

---

## Banned Words and Phrases

These fail on sight. If they appear in a draft, it needs a rewrite — not an edit.

| Category | Banned |
|----------|--------|
| Wellness jargon | nourish, wholesome, gut journey, wellness routine, microbiome-friendly (without context), supercharge, optimize |
| Corporate warmth | excited to share, thrilled, delighted, proud to announce, we believe |
| Diet culture | guilt-free, clean, cheat day, healthy indulgence, macro-friendly |
| Generic health | supports gut health (without mechanism), boosts immunity, aids digestion (without specifics) |
| Significance inflation | game-changer, revolutionary, life-changing, transformative, next level |
| AI slop signals | unlock, elevate, harness, leverage (in marketing context), seamlessly, innovative solution |

## Content Pillar Voice Notes

### Fiber + Gut Health Education
The goal: make FOS65 feel understandable and interesting, not clinical. Teach one thing per post. The test: would someone screenshot this to send to a friend? Good framing: "Here's what nobody told me about fiber..." or "The reason most fiber supplements don't work the way you think..."

### Lifestyle + Recipes
Real kitchen energy. Not food blogger energy. The swap should feel obvious once you see it. Specificity matters: "saves 12g of sugar vs. maple syrup in this recipe" beats "a healthier swap." Don't force it — if Olilo doesn't make sense in the recipe, don't use it.

### Korean Fermentation Origin
This is Joon and Rich's actual cultural heritage and the functional tradition behind the product. It earns its place when it adds meaning — "this is where we come from, this is what we know about fermentation, this is how it connects to what we built." It doesn't earn its place as a brand badge. Never lead with ethnicity as differentiation. Lead with the story.

### Community
Real people, real results, honest reactions. User content should stay in user voice — don't over-polish quotes or testimonials. If someone said "my stomach just feels better," keep it. Don't translate it into "supports digestive comfort."

---

## Tone Spectrum

**Too cold (avoid):** "Olilo contains FOS65, a fructooligosaccharide that acts as a prebiotic, feeding beneficial gut bacteria to support microbiome diversity."

**Too warm/fluffy (avoid):** "We made something special for your gut, your taste buds, and your whole beautiful life. Nourish yourself with every drop."

**On target:** "FOS65 is the fiber your gut bacteria actually eat — not you. So the sweetness is real, the calories aren't. That was the whole point when we built this."

## Visual Voice (for content concept reviews)

- Real kitchens, not studios
- Natural light, not ring lights
- Human hands in frame
- Food-forward: the syrup in use, not on a pedestal
- Warm tones, lived-in spaces
- No white clinical backgrounds
- No stock wellness imagery (woman laughing at salad, etc.)
- No flat-lay perfection — slight imperfection reads as real

---

## Scoring Shortcut

When reviewing Olilo content, ask three questions:
1. Would Joon actually say this to someone at a farmers market? (Founder test)
2. Does this teach something specific, or just gesture at health? (Education test)
3. Could this sentence appear on a Whole Foods shelf tag? (Jargon test — if yes, rewrite)

All three pass = ready for scoring. Any fail = rewrite before scoring.
